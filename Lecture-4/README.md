In lecture 4 we discussed basic MD analysis with VMD and popular Python packages (MDAnalysis and MDTraj). We specifically discussed how to calculate distances, angles, and dihedrals with VMD, and the dangers of psuedo-replication in our distributions if we naively use all of our data. To correct for this, we introduced the idea of autocorrelation, and that data needs to be sufficiently uncorrelated. Next, we discussed RMSD and RMSF, and how these values depend strongly on how you align your simulation. Lastly, we discussed dimensionality reduction with PCA, and we got "under the hood" to understand what PCA is actually doing and how it related to our covariance matrix.
